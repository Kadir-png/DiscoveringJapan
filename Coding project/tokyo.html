Tokyo, the heart of japan.

History

The history of Tokyo dates back to the twelfth century when people knew this great town was once Edo. Towards the beginning part of the 17th century, it turned over its responsibilities to the Tokugawa shogunate and became just another political center in Japan. In 1868, a new name was needed because the Meiji Restoration gave birth to what would grow into one of the world’s most important cities that we see today!

Top Attractions

  • Tokyo Tower: A symbol of Japan's post-war rebirth, offering panoramic views of the city.
  • Shinjuku Gyoen: A large park featuring traditional Japanese gardens and cherry blossoms.
  • Senso-ji Temple: Tokyo's oldest temple, located in the historic Asakusa district.
  • Akihabara: The go-to place for anime, manga, and electronics enthusiasts.
  • Tsukiji Fish Market: A world-famous market where you can experience Tokyo's vibrant food culture.

Regional Cuisine

Tokyo can range from tiny ramen shops to restaurants with Michelin stars. When you visit, make sure you try the tonkatsu, tempura, and sushi.

Cultural Customs

Tokyo places great importance on cleanliness, timeliness, and regard for others. Also, guests must understand the significance of bowing as a symbol of respect and the custom of taking off shoes before entering their homes.
